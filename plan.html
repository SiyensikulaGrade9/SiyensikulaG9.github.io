Impulse and Momentum

Impulse and momentum are two essential concepts in physics that describe how objects move and interact with each other. Impulse is the product of force and time, while momentum is the product of an object's mass and velocity. Together, these concepts help us understand how objects respond to forces and how they change their motion in response to those forces. They are crucial in many fields, from engineering to sports, and have significant practical applications. In this video we will explore the fundamental principles of impulse and momentum, including their units, calculations, and real-world applications.
